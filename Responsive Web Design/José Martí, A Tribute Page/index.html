José Martí

Un revolucionario, un patriota, un héroe

Let's give a look at José Martí's biography:

  • 1853 - José Julián Martí Pérez was born in Havana to Spanish parents
  • 1869 - He published his first political writings and Abdala, a patriotic drama in verse. Arrested for treason, he was incarcerated before being exiled to Spain
  • 1874 - After graduating in Civil Law and Canon Law in Zaragoza, he travelled to Paris before leaving for Mexico
  • 1876 - In opposition to the Plan de Tuxtepec, proclaimed by General Porfirio Díaz, Martí began collaborating with the newspaper El Socialista, favorable to Lerdo de Tejada's government. He published his article Alea Jacta Est, critical towards the Porfiristas' golpe
  • 1877 - In Guatemala, he was invited to General Garcia Granados' gatherings and fell in love with Maria, the Guatemalan president's daughter. Being already engaged to Carmen Zayas Bazán, Martí couldn't act on his love for María, who died in 1878 of lung disease, shortly after learning that Martí had married. For her, he wrote his 1891 poem La Niña de Guatemala
  • 1878 - Martí returned to Cuba, where he signed the Pact of Zanjón which ended the Cuban Ten Years' War. There, he married Carmen Zayas Bazán, before plunging into radical efforts
  • 1881 - After a brief stay in New York, Martí travelled to Caracas, Venezuela, where he founded the Revista Venezolana. In New York, Martí joined General Calixto García's Cuban revolutionary committee and worked as a journalist for several Central American journals. He also served as a consul for Uruguay, Argentina, and Paraguay
  • 1891 - His essay Nuestra America was published in New York's Revista Ilustrada. In New York, he actively participated in the Conferencia Monetaria Internacional
  • 1892 - Martí took part in a meeting of the emigration representatives in Cayo Hueso, where he contributed to the organisation of the newly formed Partido Revolucionario. To raise support and collect funding for the independence movement, he visited tobacco factories, where he gave speeches to rally workers around the cause
  • 1893 - After having travelled through the United States, Central America and the West Indies, to promote his cause and collect funds, Martí had an interview with Máximo Gómez with whom he planned the uprising, before meeting General Antonio Maceo Grajales
  • 1894 - He organized the armed expedition that would begin the Cuban revolution
  • 1895 - He died at 42 years old, fighting against Spanish troops in the battle of Dos Ríos, Cuba
For further information, consult his Wikipedia entry.
